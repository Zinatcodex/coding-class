Meaning and significance of rainbows

Meaning of a Rainbow

The word 'Rainbow' come from the Old English word 'regnboga' which is derived from the words 'regn' meaning 'rain' and 'boga' meaning 'anything bent or arched'

A rainbow is a multi-coloured arc that is formed by light striking water droplets.
Rainbows appear as perfect arcs often during a rainstorm when the sun shines onto water droplets,shattering white light into an array of brilliant colours.

The pattern of colours on a rainbow is known as a spectrum.

Significance of rainbows
  • A rainbow symbolizes the convenant between God and the Earth not to destroy it again with flood
  • Rainbows are a symbol of hope to many cultures
  • A rainbow represents inclusivity and diversity, an all-embracing image of love and friendship.
  • A rainbow symbolizes beauty after the storm to some cultures
  • A rainbow reflects the beauty and majesty of God
  • Rainbows are fun
  • Rainbows have been associated with death of pets and soldiers in war
The colours of the rainbow are:
  • Red
  • Orange
  • Yellow
  • Green
  • Blue
  • Indigo and
  • Violet
